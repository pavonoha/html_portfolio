About Me

My name is Pham Vo Ngoc Han. I was born and raised in Vietnam, and for the past five years I have been living in Texas, USA. I recently graduated with a Bachelor of Science in Information Systems from The University of Texas at Arlington. I am someone who is always willing to learn, take responsibility, and give my best effort in everything I do.

I am passionate about Artificial Intelligence and curious about how it will shape the future. During my time at UTA, I worked as a student researcher on a project developing a language translation app using AI. For me, this was more than just translating words—it was about breaking communication barriers, connecting people, and showing how technology can make life better. I also enjoy planning, problem-solving, and constantly thinking of new ideas to improve systems or fix issues.

Beyond research, I gained professional experience as a Math Tutor at Tarrant County College for 2 years, where I developed patience, communication, and mentoring skills. I love traveling, meeting new people, and exploring opportunities to grow. Although public speaking makes me nervous if I am unprepared, I see it as a challenge to push myself and become more confident. I value respect, collaboration, and bringing a friendly attitude to any team I am part of.

My long-term goal is to build a career in technology, with a focus on AI and systems that improve people’s lives. Whether it’s generating new ideas, finding bugs to fix, or creating smarter solutions, I bring curiosity, dedication, and a drive to always keep improving. I want to contribute in ways that make technology not only powerful, but also meaningful for the people who use it.
